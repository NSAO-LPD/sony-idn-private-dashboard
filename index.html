Executive Summary

The SONY Indonesia warehouse (CAK_SNYY) is operating at 39.4% space utilization — structurally healthy — but is experiencing compounding inventory performance failures across three interconnected dimensions: inbound execution, stock velocity, and outbound service level. Only 48% of goods receipts arrive on schedule (target: 80%), creating upstream buffer stock inflation. The Main stock inventory turnover collapsed from 25.4× (2025) to 3.76× (2026) — a 85% deterioration — driven by a persistent inbound surplus of 57,261 pieces over the analysis period. The SI-to-Load SLA (64.2%) shows an improving trajectory but remains below the 80% threshold. 19.1% of stock (16,815 pcs) has been idle for over 180 days, representing a write-down risk ahead of the audit period.

01
Root Cause: Supply Chain Upstream Failures

The 48% on-time GR rate is the upstream trigger of nearly every downstream problem. When goods arrive 6.5 days late on average (vs planned), the warehouse cannot maintain predictable inventory buffers. This creates two failure modes simultaneously.

First, rush-receiving pressure: when multiple late shipments arrive simultaneously, handling speed increases and damage rates spike (Apr'25: 90 units, Oct'25: 98 units). Second, overcompensation ordering: procurement teams order earlier and in larger quantities to hedge against late arrivals, directly inflating the inbound-outbound surplus.

Root cause: Supplier/carrier lead time unreliability, not warehouse capacity constraints. The Average Delay of 6.5 days suggests systemic carrier performance issues, not random variation.

02
Root Cause: Inventory Replenishment Policy Misalignment

The 57,261-piece net surplus over 11 months is not random — it reflects a reorder point calculation that was calibrated for the 2025 turnover environment (25.4×) and has not been adjusted for the current demand environment (3.76× in 2026).

This is a classic Lean Six Sigma "Variation in Demand vs Replenishment Rate" problem. When sell-through velocity drops without a corresponding pull-back in order frequency or quantities, stock inevitably accumulates. The DIO increase from 83 to 129 days (+55%) is the mathematical consequence.

Root cause: Static reorder parameters that have not tracked the shift in SONY sales velocity. Likely caused by product lifecycle changes (model transitions, market saturation) not yet reflected in WMS/ERP planning parameters.

03
Root Cause: Outbound Execution — Structural, Not Operational

The SI-to-Load SLA improvement (41% → 83%) over 11 months is the most positive finding in this analysis. It demonstrates that the warehouse team has inherent capacity to execute at high speed — the early-period failures were not a manpower or equipment constraint.

The April 2025 baseline of 41% was likely caused by WMS migration impact (stock migrated April 13, 2025) creating system reconciliation overhead that slowed physical operations. By Feb'26, the team has absorbed the migration and is operating near target.

Conclusion: Outbound SLA is a solved problem with continuing momentum. Management focus should pivot to GR on-time rate (structural) and stock clearance (immediate audit risk).

04
Data Gaps That Must Be Resolved Before Audit

Two critical metrics cannot be computed from the current WMS extract: Stock Discrepancy Rate (requires physical cycle count vs WMS balance comparison) and Picking Accuracy (requires scan error log or GI discrepancy data).

While the WMS M³ data integrity is strong (0.9% variance), unit-level accuracy is not validated. For audit purposes, an unvalidated discrepancy rate is a significant liability. A targeted cycle count of the >365d aging items (1,457 pcs) would serve both the discrepancy rate measurement and write-down assessment simultaneously.

Action: Schedule cycle count by March 21, 2026. Prioritize MAIN sub-inventory items aged >365 days and any CABN (Cargo Abnormally) items.

What Is Already Working Well

SI-to-Load SLA improvement (41% → 83%): The warehouse team has demonstrated clear operational improvement over 11 months. April 2025 was an anomaly (likely WMS migration impact). By February 2026, the team is approaching the 80% target. This should be recognized and the momentum maintained.

Space Utilization (39.4%): The warehouse is not capacity-constrained. 60.6% of M³ capacity remains available, providing structural runway for volume growth without new infrastructure investment. This is a competitive advantage.

Demo/Grade-B/Repair velocity improving: DEMO (DIO 108d → 20d), GDSB (DIO 75d → 10d), and REPR (DIO 52d → 5d) all show dramatic DIO improvements in 2026, indicating successful clearance programs in these categories. Apply the same approach to MAIN stock.
